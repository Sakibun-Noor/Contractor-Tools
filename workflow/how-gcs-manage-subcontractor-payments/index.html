How General Contractors Manage Subcontractor Payments

Sub-payment workflows are where projects bleed cash quietly. Here's the disciplined process modern GCs follow.

1. Subcontract setup

Every sub gets a defined contract value, schedule of values (SOV), and retainage terms. Procore, RedTeam, and Sage 300 CRE all model this natively.

Procore →RedTeam →Sage 300 CRE →

2. Monthly billing intake

Subs submit pay apps against the SOV. Procore Invoicing, GCPay, and Textura (now Oracle) are common platforms. DocuSign streamlines signatures.

Procore →DocuSign →

3. Lien waiver collection

Conditional + unconditional lien waivers tracked per billing cycle. Missing waivers stall payment — and create real legal exposure.

Procore →DocuSign →

4. PM approval workflow

PM verifies work-in-place vs billed-to-date. Procore and RedTeam route through approvers automatically.

Procore →RedTeam →

5. Accounting cut & release

Accounting integrates with Sage 300 CRE, Foundation Software, QuickBooks Enterprise, or CMiC to issue checks/ACH.

Sage 300 CRE →Foundation Software →QuickBooks Enterprise →CMiC →

6. Audit trail & 1099s

Year-end reporting needs clean records. The platforms above all generate the necessary reports.

Foundation Software →Sage 300 CRE →

Takeaway

A clean sub-payment workflow protects margin, protects the schedule (subs that get paid on time show up), and protects against liens. The platforms above pay for themselves on a single mid-size project.
