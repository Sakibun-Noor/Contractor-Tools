Building a Small-Contractor Tech Stack from Scratch

You're a 1-5 employee contractor. Here's the minimum viable software stack — what to buy, what to skip, and the order to deploy.

1. Field service / CRM (week 1)

Start with one platform that handles scheduling, dispatching, quoting, and invoicing. Jobber or Housecall Pro for most trades. Skip enterprise FSM until you're past 10 employees.

Jobber →Housecall Pro →

2. Accounting (week 1)

QuickBooks Online. Connect it to your FSM the same week. Don't wait — backfilling accounting is brutal.

QuickBooks Online →

3. Payments (week 2)

Mobile card processing built into your FSM. Stripe or Square as backup. Offer financing via Payzer if your average ticket is over $1k.

Stripe →Square →Payzer →

4. Reviews (week 2)

NiceJob is the easiest entry. Podium if you want SMS-first. Don't skip this — your Google rating is your CAC.

NiceJob →Podium →

5. Communication (week 3)

Slack for the team, Google Workspace for email + docs. Skip Microsoft 365 unless you already use it.

Slack →Google Workspace →

6. Time tracking (month 2)

ClockShark or Connecteam — both have geofencing and clean QuickBooks integration.

ClockShark →Connecteam →

7. Marketing (month 3)

Mailchimp for email lists, Canva for graphics, WordPress for the website. Total stack cost: ~$50/mo combined.

Mailchimp →Canva →WordPress →

Takeaway

Total monthly cost for this entire stack runs roughly $200-400/mo at the small-business scale. Add tools as the business grows — don't front-load.
