Strengths & trade-offs

Microsoft Project

Strengths

  • +Robust project scheduling and resource management across multiple sites.
  • +Purpose-built for projectmanagement workflows.
  • +Tuned to the way gc contractors actually operate.
  • +Scales to multi-office operations without breaking down at higher volume.

Trade-offs

  • −Requires admin time to configure for your trade-specific workflow.
  • −Overhead can outweigh value at very small scale.

Primavera P6

Strengths

  • +As the gold standard for complex CPM (Critical Path Method) scheduling.
  • +Purpose-built for projectmanagement workflows.
  • +Tuned to the way commercial contractors actually operate.
  • +Scales to multi-office operations without breaking down at higher volume.

Trade-offs

  • −Pricing is enterprise — not designed for solo operators.
  • −Steeper learning curve; expect 2-6 weeks of onboarding.
  • −Less battle-tested outside commercial contractors.

Bottom line

If price is the deciding factor, Microsoft Project sits in the more affordable band. Both serve commercial contractors well; the choice usually comes down to feature depth and team size.
